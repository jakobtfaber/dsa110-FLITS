\subsection{Independent CHIME-side dispersion measures}
\label{sec:chime-dm}

The DM-agreement pillar of the co-detection association (Pillar~2) requires a CHIME-side DM measured
\emph{independently} of the DSA pipeline. We measure it directly from the CHIME baseband by coherently
dedispersing each burst at its DSA DM and fitting the residual dispersion across the band. After
coherent dedispersion we downsample uniformly (a factor of $32$ in time, six sub-bands across
$0.4$--$0.8\,\mathrm{GHz}$) and, in each sub-band that exceeds $\mathrm{S/N}=4$, fit a
scattering-deconvolved exponentially-modified Gaussian to recover the intrinsic arrival time $t_0$.
A weighted linear regression of $t_0$ against $K_{\mathrm{DM}}(\nu^{-2}-\nu_{\mathrm{ref}}^{-2})$,
with $K_{\mathrm{DM}}=4.1488\times10^{3}\,\mathrm{MHz^{2}\,pc^{-1}\,cm^{3}\,s}$, returns the residual
DM offset from the DSA value as its slope. Crucially, the arrival-time regression is immune to the
$\mathrm{S/N}$-maximizing bias that drives a structure-blind dedispersion to over-dedisperse a
one-sided scattering tail: the sub-band fit centroids track the scattering-deconvolved arrival, not
the asymmetric tail's leading edge.

For $8/12$ sightlines this independently constrains the CHIME DM, all consistent with the DSA value
within a $1\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$ physical agreement floor (Fig.~\ref{fig:chime-dm-grid}); the
measured offsets cluster at $\Delta\mathrm{DM}\equiv\mathrm{DM_{CHIME}}-\mathrm{DM_{DSA}}\in[-0.84,+0.04]\,
\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$, with no sightline showing the spurious $+2$--$3\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$ excess
that a na\"ive $\mathrm{S/N}$-maximizing DM produces on the faint, scatter-broadened bursts. The
common small \emph{negative} offset across independent sightlines is signal, not noise. The remaining
$4/12$ are honest non-detections---fewer than three sub-bands clear the detection gate at the coarsest
binning the bright control still resolves---and rely on the positional pillar (Pillar~4) instead. For
the three-to-four sub-band fits the formal statistical $\sigma$ is a lower bound, so the
$1\,\mathrm{pc\,cm^{-3}}$ floor, not the quoted $\sigma$, governs the consistency test.
